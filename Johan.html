dictionaries¶

A Python dictionary is a mapping of unique keys to values. Dictionaries are mutable, which means they can be changed. To create a dictionary add your content in curled brackets( {} ) then separate your variable from it's value with a colon (:).

In [24]:
my_dictionary = {
    "first":"first value",
    "second":"second value",
    "third":"third value",
    "fourth":"fourth value",
    "fifth":"fifth value"
}
#To call value of your dictionary variable just index your variable.
my_dictionary["first"]
Out[24]:
